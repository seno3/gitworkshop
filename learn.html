Women who have been rocking the world:

Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace (1815-1852) is considered the first computer programmer. Even though she wrote about a computer, the Analytical Engine, that was never built, she realized that the computer could follow a series of simple instructions and had applications beyond pure calculation.

Grace Hopper

Grace Brewster Murray Hopper (1906-1992) was a computer pioneer and naval officer. She earned a master’s degree (1930) and a Ph.D. (1934) in mathematics from Yale. Hopper is best known for her trailblazing contributions to computer programming, software development, and the design and implementation of programming languages.

Margaret Hamilton

Margaret Elaine Hamilton (1936-) is an American computer scientist, systems engineer, and business owner. She was director of the Software Engineering Division of the MIT Instrumentation Laboratory, which developed on-board flight software for NASA's Apollo program.

Rani Johnson

Rani Johnson, the Chief Information Officer at Workday, is a dynamic and high-impact leader renowned for her data-driven approach and expertise across multiple domains including IT operations, business applications, cybersecurity, and business transformation. She excels as a strategic partner, aligning closely with business leaders to drive technological innovation and execute robust technology roadmaps that meet strategic objectives. Rani's journey in technology commenced with a notable internship at NASA Johnson Space Center, where her team contributed code deployed on the International Space Station—an early indicator of her technical prowess. Holding dual degrees in electrical engineering and general engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology and Spelman College, Rani continues to push boundaries in technology leadership and innovation.
